Because normal human activity is worse for nature than the greatest nuclear accident in history.

Martin Cruz Smith, American Novelist

Air quality is an important factor in our environment and should thus play an important role in our lifestyle. Keeping yourself and your friends and family safe in today's world requires understanding the effects of air pollution on your body and how your lifestyle needs to adapt based on the current air quality. The Air Quality Index (AQI) is divided into several categories of severity, these are "Good", "Moderate", "Unhealthy for sensitive groups", "Unhealthy", "Very Unhealthy" and "Hazardous". Depending on which category your current air quality falls into, you should take precautionary measures.

Air Quality Categories

AQI Category AQI range Health Precautions
Good 0 to 50 This level of Air Pollution inflicts little to no harm to anyone, it is completely safe to spend as much time as you want outdoors.
Moderate 51 to 100 This level of air pollution isn't a serious concern but may possibly cause breathing discomfort to sensitive groups. Unusually sensitive groups or those with lung diseases, e.g. asthma, should reduce prolonged strenuous tasks outdoors.
Unhealthy for sensitive groups 101 to 150 This level of air pollution causes breathing discomfort for sensitive groups though general public may not experience drastic effects. Sensitive groups should reduce prolonged strenuous tasks outdoors.
Unhealthy 151 to 200 This level of air pollution affects everyone negatively but has more severe health effects for sensitive groups. General public should reduce prolonged strenuous tasks outdoors. Sensitive groups and those with lung diseases should reduce all strenuous tasks outdoors. Pollution masks are reccommended.
Very Unhealthy 201 to 300 This level of air pollution affects everyone severely. Sensitive groups and those with lung diseases should avoid all unnecessary outdoor tasks. General public should avoid all strenuous tasks outdoors.
Hazardous 301 to 500 This level of air pollution is dangerous for sensitive groups and those with lung diseases and has severe health effects for the general public. Sensitive groups and those with lung diseases should avoid going outdoors. General public should avoid all unnecessary tasks outdoors.

General Tips

If you live in an area which is generally prone to high levels of air pollution, here are some general tips to stay safe.

  1. Pollution mask - When outdoors, if possible, keep a pollution mask on. Pollution masks are one of the easiest ways to avoid prolonged exposure to poor air quality.
  2. Avoid exercising outdoors - Strenuous exercise outdoors can be harmful to your body, instead going to a gym or exercising indoors can be beneficial.
  3. Add some plants to your home - If you're ready to take responsibility for them, having multiple plants around your home is an easy way to improve your home's air quality
  4. Install an air purifier - If your home's air quality is poor, it may be important to get a personal air purifier for your home
  5. Eat healthier - A simple yet effective strategy against air pollution is improving your immune system through eating healthier. Information about improving your immune system.
